Project description

An Arduino-based car rover is a compact robot that utilizes an Arduino microcontroller board as its central processing unit. By integrating Arduino programming with mechanical components, it becomes a dynamic mobile device capable of executing diverse tasks. The rover generally features two or more motor-driven wheels, precisely controlled by the Arduino board. Moreover, it can incorporate an array of sensors, such as ultrasonic, infrared, or camera modules, to facilitate perception and interaction with its surroundings.

Project description

A drone constructed with an Arduino Uno is a compact unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV) that relies on the Arduino Uno board as its primary flight controller. The Arduino Uno acts as the central processor, executing the flight control software and analyzing data from diverse sensors to stabilize and govern the drone's flight. By utilizing Electronic Speed Controllers (ESCs), the drone regulates the speed of its brushless motors and propellers, which generate the necessary lift and forward motion. Incorporated with an Inertial Measurement Unit (IMU) that houses a gyroscope and accelerometer, the drone can ascertain its orientation and acceleration, facilitating the Arduino's task of maintaining stability and adjusting flight behavior.
